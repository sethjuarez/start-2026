# Scenario Information

We’re telling the story of a frontier firm operating globally in the pharmaceutical sector, whose ambition is to accelerate innovation, lower cost, and improve outcomes at scale.

They are not defined by a single industry slice. While grounded in drug discovery and manufacturing, their work spans the pharmaceutical lifecycle—from research and development through production, commercialization, and market access—giving us a narrative that can extend across verticals…

At its core, this is a company committed to solving complex, real-world problems that ultimately make the world a better place: Bringing life-improving medications to market faster, scaling production responsibly, and delivering better outcomes for patients: bringing life-improving medications to market faster.

# Where they are today

The defining characteristic of this company is how they operate and the deep integration of AI across their business.

- Leaders, researchers, operators, and developers set direction and make decisions
- AI operates across the company as a continuous layer that connects work, accelerates insight, and executes behind the scenes
- This intelligence layer becomes a strategic asset, allowing them to move faster and operate more competitively in market

Multiple roles interact in a shared system, with AI helping coordinate and carry context across each step

The connected AI-native lifecycle allows them to build intelligent systems, observe and govern them in real time, and continuously optimize for performance and cost

# AI is embedded throughout the business process

Not a separate tool—it shows up inside workflows: creating artifacts, analyzing signals, coordinating actions, and automating tasks

The experience spans everything from conversational AI to autonomous execution, with security and trust integrated into the flow

# How they got here

Some months ago, <Company> made a deliberate strategic pivot to reframe how they think about AI. This pivot was grounded in a new operating philosophy: AI must do more than make us more efficient—it must make us extraordinary.

This pivot builds on three decades of disciplined growth and capability building. Founded in the early 1990s, <Company> began as a regional generics manufacturer with a clear mission: Make safe, effective medications more accessible globally. Over time, it scaled to a global enterprise operating in more than 100 countries with a diverse product portfolio, spanning over the counter (OTC), specialty therapeutics, and consumer health products.

# The demo hook

When we encounter <Company>, they are reacting to the breaking news that a direct competitor has announced a new product launch in a therapeutic category where the company already has a leading position, offering a longer-lasting injection with fewer doses and an earlier market entry timeline.

With the announcement, the expected time-to-market advantage is reduced, with the competitor expected to launch ahead of <Company>’s next-generation product, creating immediate pressure and forcing the company to adjust its operations in real time, but its current systems aren’t equipped to respond at that pace.

This forces a critical leadership question: Can <Company> respond fast enough to close the timing gap and defend market share, without introducing operational or regulatory risk?

With this backdrop, the team moves quickly to assemble a cross-functional team:

- They pull together data across the business to understand launch impact, the new competitive demand signal and timeline shift
- They identify where they can unlock operational systems to deliver at speed and scale, with supply chain and capacity emerging as the primary constraint
- They define the requirements and solution design for real-time demand response based on capacity constraints, with fully autonomous / long running agents within defined governance and control boundaries to meet the accelerated timeline

This kicks off the remaining demos, which explore how they build, secure, govern, operate, and optimize this AI-first supply chain system. We see:

- Increase batch release velocity and manufacturing throughput through AI-driven scheduling
- Capture early market share by dynamically allocating supply to high-demand markets
- Reduce time-to-first-prescription and accelerate patient access to life-improving therapies
- Maintain continuous GMP compliance and inspection readiness under compressed timelines
- Sustain competitive market leadership under accelerated competitive pressure
- Accelerate payor and formulary readiness to enable rapid market access
